## soldering for the first time

Soldering for the rist time and decided to wear shorts for the process :/
Well guess how that went!!

I got my first burn scar on the first day. Anyways, it counts for the experience; scars are a champion's markkks!! (yh sumn lyk that)

I started off by soldering the key/switches (if i remmeber correctly), and thought to myself "is this really necessaryy?"

So, I started soldering the LED's and burnt the back of two. It doesn't affect the connectivity tho!

On my second day of doldering, I melted a part of a socket in the house. Nothing terrible tho, thankfully.

I then soldered the OLED display. When ddesigning a different product 'EDGE' that I needed OLED displays for, I realised that I had designed my OLED display wrongly for the macropad. But I had already soldered the OLED :/

I maybe gave up for a week, thinking the oled would blow up if I soldered the microcontroller. well, I still think that, so I still haven't soldered my micro, which is the only thing currently remaining!!

After a couple weeks, I tried desoldering the OLED with my solder iron. I spent at lest two hours. Even after it looked like all the solder was removed, it was not!! I tried yanking her out, and it didn't work AT ALL!

I hereby am in need of flux!!!! More interestingly I have a highly oxidated :), I have maybe three bridges :) and my joints look like ummmm... :) (see pics). THEREFORE, I AM IN NEED OF FLUXXXX!

More updates coming soon!
